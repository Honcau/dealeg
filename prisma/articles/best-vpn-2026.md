A VPN encrypts your internet traffic and hides your real location, which is useful for privacy, security on public Wi-Fi, and accessing content restricted to certain regions. But the VPN market is crowded with near-identical marketing, and the real differences come down to price, speed, server coverage, and jurisdiction. This guide compares the leading providers on what actually matters and helps you pick the right one without overpaying.

## What a VPN actually does (and doesn't)

At its core, a VPN routes your connection through an encrypted tunnel to a server elsewhere, masking your IP address and hiding which sites you visit from your internet provider. That's genuinely useful for protecting yourself on public networks, keeping your browsing private from your ISP, and connecting to services as if you were in another country.

It is not, however, a magic cloak of total anonymity. A VPN hides your traffic from your ISP and your location from websites, but you still need good habits elsewhere. Treat a VPN as one strong layer of privacy and security, not a complete solution by itself.

## What separates a good VPN from a bad one

Before comparing providers, know the four factors that actually distinguish them, because the marketing won't tell you.

**An audited no-logs policy.** The whole point of a VPN is privacy, so the provider must not keep records of what you do. The key word is *audited* — independent firms verifying the no-logs claim, not just the company asserting it. Every provider worth using has passed third-party audits.

**Jurisdiction.** Where the company is legally based determines which governments can compel it to hand over data. Privacy-friendly jurisdictions outside major surveillance alliances are preferable. This is a real differentiator between otherwise similar services.

**Speed and server network.** All VPNs slow your connection somewhat; good ones barely. Modern protocols like WireGuard minimize the hit. Server count and, more importantly, server *locations* determine how well it performs for your needs — more locations mean a nearby fast server and more region options.

**Honest pricing.** Like hosting, VPNs lure you with low two-year rates that renew higher. The monthly price is almost always poor value; the long-term plans are where the real deals live — but check the renewal rate.

## The leading providers compared

### NordVPN — best all-around

NordVPN is the safest default for most people. It combines one of the largest server networks — servers across roughly 135 countries — with consistently fast speeds via its NordLynx protocol and reliable access to region-locked streaming. Its Threat Protection feature blocks malware and trackers even when the VPN is off, and it's based in a privacy-friendly jurisdiction with audited no-logs. Two-year plans land around $3.49 per month. It's not the absolute cheapest, but for the balance of speed, coverage, security features, and trust, it's the easiest recommendation. A 30-day money-back guarantee lets you test it risk-free.

### Surfshark — best value

Surfshark is the standout for price without cutting corners. Its two-year plans start around $2.49 per month, and — uniquely among top providers — it allows **unlimited simultaneous devices** on one account, making it ideal for families or anyone with many gadgets. Owned by the same parent company as NordVPN, it shares high privacy standards and audited no-logs, with a large server network and modern protocols. It's marginally behind NordVPN on streaming reliability and has fewer server countries, but for the price and the unlimited-device perk, it's exceptional value.

### ExpressVPN — best for beginners

ExpressVPN is the most polished and beginner-friendly option, with exceptionally clean apps across every platform. It's invested heavily in security, advancing post-quantum encryption in its Lightway protocol and passing more than twenty independent security audits. A notable proof point: its transparency report confirmed that despite over a million data requests, it disclosed zero user data — a track record that backs up the privacy promise. Two-year plans run about $2.49 per month. It's slightly slower than NordVPN and Surfshark in raw speed tests, but the ease of use makes it the gentlest entry into VPNs.

### Proton VPN — best for privacy

Proton VPN is the choice when privacy is the absolute priority. Run by the team behind the privacy-focused Proton email service, it offers the widest country coverage on this list, full-disk server encryption, and a Secure Core feature that routes traffic through extra-hardened servers for added protection. It's one of the fastest in independent upload-speed tests, and — uniquely — offers a genuinely good free tier with the same security as the paid plans. Best for users who put privacy and transparency above all else.

### Private Internet Access — best for power users

PIA is built for people who like to tinker. It offers an enormous server network covering all 50 US states, unlimited simultaneous devices, and an unusually deep set of configurable options — port forwarding, multiple protocols, and granular controls that advanced users love. Its no-logs policy is independently audited, and long-term pricing is among the cheapest available. Best for technical users who want maximum control and customization.

## How to choose

For **most people**, NordVPN offers the best overall balance and is the safest pick. If **value matters most** or you have many devices, Surfshark's unlimited-device plans at a lower price are unbeatable. If you've **never used a VPN** and want the simplest experience, ExpressVPN's polished apps make it the easiest start. For **maximum privacy**, Proton VPN's hardened infrastructure and free tier stand out. And for **power users** who want to configure everything, Private Internet Access delivers the most control.

Whichever you choose, the buying advice is identical across all of them: skip the expensive monthly plan, take the long-term discount, search for a working coupon before checkout, and set a reminder to reassess at renewal rather than auto-paying full price. Every provider here offers a money-back guarantee, so you can test your pick risk-free before committing.
